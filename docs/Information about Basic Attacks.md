# Basic attacks
A basic attack (also called a standard, normal, or auto attack) is the default means by which a unit deals damage. The player orders their champion to perform a basic attack when they right-click on an enemy, or when the "attack-move" command is used

Turrets, minions, and most monsters perform basic attacks as their only method of dealing damage. A basic attack under normal circumstances deals 100% AD physical damage.

A unit's attack timer determines the time it takes to fire a basic attack and wait for a new one to be available; it is reduced by attack speed and reset by basic attack resets. 


## Behavior
A basic attack cannot normally be avoided once the attacker has begun their attack. It also cannot lose its target and may hit them despite subsequent changes in position.

Exceptions that cause basic attacks to fail:
- Parrying effects (negate the attack entirely):
	- Dodge
	- Block
	- Blind
- Attacking becomes disabled, preventing basic attacks from being declared:
	- Disarming crowd control
	- Channeling
	- Lockout, including cast time

- Attack windup is interrupted if:
	- The attacker is issued a different command
	- The attacker or the target dies
	- The target becomes untargetable
	- The target moves too far away
	- The target is no longer in sight

- Physically dodgeable attacks:
Some basic attacks do not follow their target after being fired and can be dodged with mobility spells or quick movement, though they travel fast and are difficult to avoid. Examples include:
	- Graves – New Destiny
	- Twitch – Spray and Pray


## Damage
Basic attacks always deal physical damage, with some exceptions.

- The following will modify the attack's total damage to magic damage: 
	- Galio – Colossal Smash  
	- Nidalee – Takedown  
	- Sylas – Petricite Burst  
	- Twisted Fate – Pick a Card  
	- Viktor – Discharge  
	- Zac – Stretching Strikes  
	- Zeri – Living Battery  

- The following will modify the attack to be two damage types:
	- Yone – Way of the Hunter: part physical, part magic damage  
	- Camille – Empowered Precision Protocol: part physical, part true damage  


### Additional Notes
Aside from the exceptions above, certain items, buffs, and champion abilities can cause basic attacks to deal additional damage of any subtype on top of their base physical damage.  
Physical damage from basic attacks scales with the attacker's **Attack Damage** stat and is reduced by the target's **armor**.


## Attack Reset
The cast of certain abilities and item actives will (forcibly) reset the attack timer to zero. 
Depending on the phase of the attack that the player casts them in, this effect may be detrimental or beneficial to them:
	
- Resetting the attack timer during the windup means that the current attack is canceled, thus delaying the windup and launch of the next attack.
- Resetting the attack timer during the cooldown means that the remaining cooldown is negated, thus allowing a new windup to begin faster than normal.
- Hail of Blades (Rune) generates an additional stack when the user's attack timer is reset.

#### Double Attack Resets
The special attacks from the following **reset the caster's attack timer** and **do not place the user's attack timer on cooldown**, resulting in a double attack reset:  

- Leona – Shield of Daybreak  
- Volibear – Thundering Smash  

#### Dash Abilities and Attack Timing

Certain dash abilities that grant attack resets may cause the caster to dash for longer than their attack's cooldown. This can delay follow-up basic attacks.  

To reduce this lockout time, the caster may attempt to end the dash prematurely (e.g., by dashing into a wall or in place) if the ability allows. Shortening the dash reduces the time required before the next basic attack.
